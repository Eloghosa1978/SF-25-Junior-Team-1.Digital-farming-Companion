What is Seasonal Farming?

Seasonal farming means growing crops based on the time of the year. Some crops love the rain, and some love the sun. Smart farmers choose the best crops for the season so their farms can be full of food!

Types of Seasons

There are four main types of seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Each season has its own weather patterns and suitable crops. But we would be treating the two main type of seasons wet and dry

Wet Season

The wet season is when it rains a lot. This is the best time to plant crops that need water, like rice and vegetables. Farmers use the rain to help their plants grow big and healthy.

Benefits of Wet Season Farming

  • Best time to plant water-loving crops examples include;
    1. Rice
    2. Maize
    3. Beans
    4. Sweet Potatoes
    5. Yams
  • Utilizes rain for crop growth
  • Farmers save water for plants
  • Helps plants grow big and healthy

Dry Season

The dry season is when it doesn‚Äôt rain much. Farmers plant crops that can survive without much water, like maize and beans. They also save water for their plants during this time.

Benefits of Dry Season Farming

  • Best time to plant crops that can survive without much water, examples include;
    1. Maize
    2. Beans
    3. Groundnuts
    4. Okra
    5. Tomatoes
  • Farmers save water for plants
  • Helps plants grow strong and healthy

Reasons Why Seasonal Farming Is Important

  1. Better Crop Growth: Plants grow better when they are planted at the right time of year.
  2. More Food: Seasonal farming helps farmers grow more food for everyone.
  3. Less Water Waste: Farmers use rainwater during the wet season, so they don‚Äôt waste water.
  4. Healthy Plants: Plants that grow in the right season are healthier and stronger.
  5. Helps Farmers Earn Money: When farmers grow more food, they can sell it and earn money.

  2. Learn Continuously: Stay updated with the latest farming techniques and practices.
