Meet Our Farm Animals

Did you know? Farm animals provide us with food, clothing, and help farmers with their work! Let's meet some of them.

Cows

Cows give us milk that we use to make cheese, yogurt, and ice cream! A dairy cow can produce about 30 liters of milk each day.

Chickens

Chickens lay eggs - about 250 per year! They also help control insects in the farm. Chickens are related to dinosaurs!

Pigs

Pigs are very smart animals. They help recycle food waste by eating scraps. Pigs roll in mud to protect their skin from sunburn.

Sheep

Sheep give us wool to make warm clothes. A sheep can produce enough wool each year to make 10 sweaters!

Farm Activities

Morning Chores

Farmers wake up early to feed the animals, collect eggs, and milk the cows. Animals always eat breakfast before farmers do!

Animal Care

Farmers check animals every day to make sure they're healthy. They brush them, clean their homes, and call the vet when needed.

Harvest Time

At harvest time, farmers collect wool, milk, eggs, and other products. Then they prepare them for stores and markets.
